Barcelona has a lot of things to do and places to visit. It’s culture and self-indulgent of gourmands are interesting. The only thing is, those entry fees and huge bills. Fortunately, help is one of the way to make your holiday budget a bit stretchable. Here are some free activities and sight which you can enjoy at your fullest in Barcelona. Besides this, you can even save money in transport by buying T10 travel card from which you can travel 10 trips within the city through any form of public transport.

  1. FREE MUSEUMS ON SUNDAYS

    2. Some city-run museums including the Museu Picasso and MUHBA are free on Sunday from 3pm to 8pm.
    Other museums are also free on one certain day of the month. It is often on the first Sunday or Wedneday.

  2. VISIT FOR A FESTIVAL

    3. If you are going there in late September then you can go to five day Festes de la Merce. This brings the city to life with open concerts, dancing, fireeworks, acrobatic feats and lively corredocs-colourful parades.
    Otherwise if you are going there during summer then you can have pleasure with extravaganza Festa Major de Gràcia. This festival is best known for its competition of decorated streets. You can also enjoy free outdoor concert during this festival.

  3. WALKING IN LA RAMBLA

    4. La Rambla is a great place to walk around particularly during morning. It is lined with regal historical buildings.
    It is like a most for every tourist to walk along this 1km long path to experience Barcelona.

  4. VISIT THE MERCAT DE LA BOQUERIA

    5. This is famous indoor market hall full of colourful fruits, vegetables, seafood, rows and rows of cured jamón and some mind-boggling butchers’ displays. It also include tapas bars, pizza stalls and all manner of produce where you can try before you buy the food.

  5. APPRECIATE MODERNISME ARCHITECTURE

    6. Most of the Barcelona's architectural gems have entry fee to see the interior but the most impressive facades can be seen for free. For example, the cathedral- La Sagrada Familia; mind-blowing craftsmanship of Gaudi's magnum work of art.
    The Casa Lleó Morera, the Casa Amatller and Gaudí’s Casa Batlló are attractive examples of modernisme which lies side by side on the Passeig de Gràcia

  6. SUNBATH ON BEACH

    7. Barcelona has wonderful beaches suitable for resting after tired days of sightseeing.
    Barceloneta Beach is the most popular beach in Barcelona with lovely golden sand and seafront restaurants at thne end of beach.

  7. STARE AT JOAN MIRO'S PUBLIC ART

    8. There are many interesting Joan Miró scluptures around the city which acn be viewed for free. You can also see 22m tall Woman and bird sclupture covered in primary coloured tiles and rising dramatically from a sparkling pool. There is also a Miró mosaic in the center walkway of La Rambla and even on the external wall of Terminal II at Barcelona Airport.

  8. BECOME ASTONISHED BY LA CATEDRAL

    9. In the heart of Barri Gòtic, there is very attractive the colossal neo-Gothic La Catedral. you can have free entry inside during morning and late afternoon. You can become astonished by its domed ceilings,pillars and cloister with courtyard of palms, orange trees and resident gaggle of white geese.

  9. BE DELIGHTED AT THE FONT MAGICA

    10. This is build for Barcelona's 1929 World Exposition. This has been the most attractive part of Barcelona for tourists due to it's water, sound and lightshow.
    If ypu don't like crowd then you can further walk north towards the Fòrum area.
